Once I arrived at Western I realized it was time to get to work. I moved into Nash Hall and started working towards my degree. However, the first step was figuring out the degree I was going to pursue. I went through a couple options; journalism, kinesiology, and business. Eventually I landed on business and as you will see the rest is history. At the info fair I saw a club that caught my attention and I immediately applied. The club being a professional development fraternity called Alpha Kappa Psi. I joined the club through a rigorous 8 week program where I learned how to interview, how to build my resume, presentation skills, and how to grow professionally. As the year progressed I found new friends through flag football and worked on my many new classes. Learning more about business and completing my GURs through out the first year was a very good start.
